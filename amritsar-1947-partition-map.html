AMRITSAR 1947

Mapping the Partition Violence — March to September

Filter Events by Month

MARCH 1947 — The Conflagration Begins

March 2: Khizr Hayat Tiwana resigns as Premier under Muslim League pressure.

March 3: Master Tara Singh brandishes kirpan at Punjab Assembly, shouts "Pakistan Murdabad."

March 4: Hindu-Sikh demonstrations against Pakistan demand. Clashes erupt.

March 5 (Holi): Violence explodes in Hall Bazaar. Sharifpura train attack. Hall Bazaar, Katra Jaimal Singh destroyed. "Tall pillars of smoke seen from 5 miles away."

March 6: Chowk Pragdas mosque attack. Sikh milkmen massacred near Hathi Gate (18+ bodies to mortuary). British troops brought in on 7th.

Official toll by March 16: Punjab-wide 1,046 killed, 1,167 injured. Amritsar among worst affected.

APRIL 1947 — The Lull Breaks

After a relative calm following the March outbreak, violence resurges on April 11 (Friday).

A Muslim crowd of 4,000-5,000 comes to Chowk Pragdas for Friday prayers, but they are armed and shouting revenge slogans. After prayers, some attack Sikhs, triggering renewed rioting.

April 11: Sikh grinding mill gutted by Muslim mob. One Sikh fired upon by police without cause.

Throughout April, stabbing incidents continue in alleys and bazaars. Both communities arm themselves.

Context: Violence in Amritsar and Lahore "bounced off each other"—a flare-up in one city triggered reprisals in the other.

MAY 1947 — Systematic Killings

May 2: Sikh stabbed outside Hakiman Gate.

May 10: The Daimganj Idgah attack—7 Hindus and Sikhs returning from a child's cremation are ambushed, killed, and their bodies soaked in petrol and set ablaze. The smoke attracts police from Amritsar Fort.

May 21: Sikh found murdered in Gol Bagh between Baijnath High School and Railway Footbridge.

May 26: Muslim Sub-Inspector chases Hindu from Islamabad Railway Crossing to Khalsa College, firing repeatedly. One Hindu murdered in Chitta Katra.

Locations with multiple killings: 9 Sikhs killed outside Bhagtanwala Gate, 7 in Daimganj, 30 outside Rambagh Gate, 12 outside Sharifpura.

Key areas: Sharifpura, Mahan Singh Gate, Hathi Gate, Bhagtanwala Gate, and Chauk Farid were "especially notorious" for attacks on Sikhs.

JUNE 1947 — Heroes Amid Horror

June 3: Mountbatten announces Partition Plan. Punjab to be divided.

June 23: Punjab Legislative Assembly votes on partition. Western section votes against (69-27), Eastern section votes for (50-22).

While violence continues, remarkable acts of humanity emerge:

Dr. Abdur Rauf in Katra Karamsingh saves 200 cornered non-Muslims by invoking Islamic teachings.

Bawa Ghansham, a Sikh Communist, shelters hundreds of Muslims at great personal risk.

Context: Violence "on a relatively lower scale" compared to March, but tension mounts as Partition becomes inevitable.

JULY 1947 — Jathas Organize

July 10: Giani Kartar Singh issues an ultimatum to Governor Jenkins—if Sikh demands not met, they will fight by "murdering officials, cutting railway lines and telegraph lines, destroying canal headworks."

July 11: "General" Mohan Singh tells Jenkins the only solution is "a very substantial exchange of population." If it doesn't happen, "the Sikhs would be driven to facilitate it by a massacre of Muslims."

July 30: Jenkins reports to Mountbatten: "a string of rural outrages" in Amritsar district. Muslims murdered "in various ways." "The aggressors in all cases seem to be the Sikhs."

Sikh jatha strength estimated at 19,000. Preparations intensify.

Jenkins' warning: "There is going to be trouble with the Sikhs. When, and how bad, the Governor cannot yet say."

AUGUST 1947 — The Great Cleansing

August 1: Jenkins' urgent telegram to Mountbatten: jathas targeting Muslim villages around Amritsar. Nearly two dozen Muslims killed and 30 wounded in 48 hours. Four trains attacked.

August 14-15: Independence. Mass Muslim exodus from Amritsar. "Within the space of a day and a night, almost no Muslim was to be seen in Amritsar when freedom dawned—except those who had entrenched themselves in Sharifpura."

August 17: Radcliffe Award announced. Amritsar goes to India. Sikhs and Hindus "celebrate their victory."

August 25 (The Times): "The Sikhs are on the war-path. They are clearing eastern Punjab of Muslims, butchering hundreds daily... Some large towns like Amritsar are now quieter, because there are no Muslims left."

Amritsar becomes transit point for 250,000+ Muslim refugees heading to Lahore. Crowds chant "No Muslims out!"

Nehru to Gandhi, August 22: "Twice as many Muslims had been killed in East Punjab as Hindus and Sikhs in the West."

SEPTEMBER 1947 — The Ghost Trains

Early September: 1,000 Muslim refugees massacred on a Pakistan-bound train near Khalsa College.

September 22: THE GREAT TRAIN MASSACRE. A train carrying 4,000+ Muslims is attacked at Amritsar for three hours. 3,000 killed, 1,000 wounded. Only 100 remain uninjured. The British commanding officer is killed—reportedly by his own men after ordering them to fire on attackers.

Late September: Pakistan stops all trains from East Punjab. The Sikh jathas are described as "ruthless," leading attacks for "ethnically cleansing" East Punjab.

Violence continues into October before "petering out by mid-October."

Final context: Amritsar district 46.5% Muslim pre-Partition. By October, zero Muslims remain in most areas.
